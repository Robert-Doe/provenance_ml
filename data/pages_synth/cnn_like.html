As funding talks stall, agencies sketch contingency plans

By Jamie Park September 3, 2025 Location: Washington

Agencies across the federal government have begun drafting contingency plans as talks over a stopgap funding measure drag into the week, according to officials familiar with the discussions.

“There’s urgency without panic,” one senior aide said, describing a sprint to line up votes while avoiding last-minute surprises.

The emerging framework would keep spending flat for several weeks while committees hammer out differences over policy riders. Several lawmakers cautioned that even a short extension could be complicated by amendments on unrelated issues that have vocal constituencies.
